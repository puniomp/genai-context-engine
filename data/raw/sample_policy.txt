This policy establishes guidelines for the responsible development, deployment, and operation of artificial intelligence systems within the organization. The purpose of this document is to ensure that AI technologies are used in a manner that aligns with company values, regulatory obligations, and ethical standards. All employees, contractors, and partners involved in designing or operating AI systems must adhere to the principles outlined in this policy. These guidelines apply to both internally developed AI systems and third-party AI services integrated into company products or workflows.
Artificial intelligence systems must be designed with transparency and accountability in mind. Teams deploying AI models should document the purpose of the system, the datasets used during training, and the expected outputs. Documentation should also describe known limitations, potential biases, and operational constraints. Maintaining clear documentation ensures that stakeholders understand how the system functions and allows for easier auditing and troubleshooting when issues arise.
Data used to train or operate AI systems must comply with all applicable data governance and privacy regulations. Sensitive information such as personally identifiable information, protected health data, or financial records must only be used in accordance with approved data handling procedures. Where possible, datasets should be anonymized or de-identified prior to model training or evaluation. Data access must be restricted to authorized personnel who require it for legitimate business purposes.
Organizations must implement safeguards to reduce the risk of harmful or biased outcomes from AI systems. Prior to deployment, models should undergo evaluation processes designed to detect bias, fairness issues, or unintended behaviors. If a system is found to produce discriminatory or harmful outputs, teams must either mitigate the issue or suspend deployment until the risk has been addressed. Responsible AI practices require continuous monitoring and improvement of deployed models.
All AI systems deployed in production environments must include mechanisms for monitoring performance and usage. Operational metrics such as latency, error rates, and response quality should be tracked through centralized logging and observability platforms. Monitoring allows teams to quickly detect degradation in system performance or unexpected behavior. When issues are identified, incident response procedures should be followed to restore normal operation.
Employees must exercise caution when using generative AI tools to produce content or automate workflows. Outputs generated by AI systems may contain inaccuracies or fabricated information. As a result, AI-generated content should be reviewed by a qualified human before being used in official communications, decision-making processes, or customer-facing materials. Human oversight remains an essential component of responsible AI deployment.
Access to AI systems and supporting infrastructure must follow the organization’s identity and access management policies. Authentication and authorization controls should ensure that only approved users can interact with sensitive models or datasets. Access privileges should follow the principle of least privilege, meaning users receive only the permissions necessary to perform their roles. Regular audits should be conducted to verify compliance with access control policies.
Third-party AI services must undergo security and compliance reviews before being integrated into production systems. Vendors providing AI capabilities must demonstrate adherence to relevant regulatory standards and industry best practices. Contracts with third-party providers should include provisions addressing data protection, confidentiality, and service reliability. These safeguards help ensure that external AI solutions do not introduce unnecessary risk to the organization.
Training and awareness programs should be provided to employees who interact with AI systems. These programs should educate staff on responsible AI practices, data handling requirements, and ethical considerations related to automated decision-making. Ensuring that employees understand both the capabilities and limitations of AI technologies reduces the likelihood of misuse or overreliance on automated outputs.
Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all teams deploying or interacting with AI systems. Violations may result in disciplinary action, revocation of system access, or additional corrective measures. The policy will be reviewed periodically to account for evolving regulatory requirements, technological advancements, and organizational needs. Updates will be communicated to all relevant stakeholders to ensure continued adherence to responsible AI practices.
